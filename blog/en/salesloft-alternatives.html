8 Best Salesloft Alternatives in 2026 (After the Clari Merger)

Salesloft merged with Clari in 2025. If you're rethinking your sales engagement stack, here are 8 alternatives compared on pricing,

Nicolas Finet | April 10, 2026 | 14 min read

TL;DR

The best Salesloft alternatives in 2026 are Overloop ($69/mo, multichannel outbound), Outreach.io (direct enterprise competitor), and Apollo.io (free tier + database + sequences). Salesloft merged with Clari in 2025 and is now bundled as a "Predictive Revenue System." Pricing is estimated at ~$125/user/month with annual contracts, 10-15 seat minimums, and no monthly billing option. These 8 alternatives offer transparent pricing and simpler onboarding for teams that just need outreach sequences.

Disclosure: We built Overloop, which competes with Salesloft. We're biased, and we'll tell you exactly where and why. You'll get the full picture either way.

Salesloft merged with Clari in 2025. Before that, they acquired Drift (the conversational AI platform) in 2024. The product is now positioned as a "Predictive Revenue System" that bundles sales engagement, deal management, revenue forecasting, and AI chat.

For some enterprise teams, that consolidation is exactly what they want. For others, it raises questions. Will pricing go up as Clari monetizes the combined platform? Will the engagement features get less attention as the company focuses on revenue intelligence? Will existing workflows break during integration?

If you're evaluating alternatives, whether proactively or because your contract is up, here are 8 options across different price points and use cases.

The Post-Merger Situation

Here's what changed after the Clari-Salesloft merger and why teams are exploring alternatives:

  • Pricing uncertainty. Salesloft already required custom quotes starting around $125/user/month. Multi-year contracts include 5-8% annual escalators. Post-merger, early renewal quotes suggest prices are trending upward as Clari bundles more features into required packages.
  • Platform complexity. What was a sales engagement tool is now part of a revenue intelligence platform. Teams that just need sequences and calling are paying for forecasting and conversation analytics they don't use.
  • Minimum seat requirements. Typically 10-15 users minimum depending on tier and region. Small teams can't buy Salesloft even if they want to.
  • Annual contracts only. No monthly billing. No easy way to test the platform with a real workflow before committing budget.

1. Overloop

Overloop is built for teams of 1-50 reps who want multichannel outreach without enterprise pricing or complexity. Every plan includes email, LinkedIn automation, and calling. The AI generates full outreach sequences from prospect data, not just subject line suggestions.

Compared to Salesloft, Overloop is dramatically simpler. No minimum seats. No annual contract required. No custom quotes. You sign up, connect your email and LinkedIn, and start sending. The trade-off is that Overloop doesn't do deal management, revenue forecasting, or conversation intelligence. It's a focused outreach tool.

2. Outreach.io

If Salesloft is one side of the enterprise sales engagement coin, Outreach is the other. Pricing is similarly opaque (expect $100-160/user/month), contracts are annual, and the platform is built for large teams with dedicated RevOps support.

The main reason to switch from Salesloft to Outreach is specific feature preferences or better terms. Outreach has stronger A/B testing and analytics in some areas. But this is a lateral move between platforms at the same tier. If your problem with Salesloft is price or complexity, Outreach won't solve it.

3. Apollo.io

Apollo gives you a 275M+ contact database and outreach sequencing in one platform. The Basic plan at $49/user/month (annual) includes email sequences, 900 mobile credits/year, and CRM integration.

For teams that currently use Salesloft for engagement and a separate tool (like ZoomInfo or Lusha) for prospecting, Apollo can replace both. The credit system means heavy users pay more than the sticker price, but the combined value often beats paying for two separate platforms.

Read more: Apollo.io Alternatives

4. Reply.io

Reply.io is the most feature-complete multichannel tool in the mid-market bracket. The base Multichannel plan at $89/month covers email, with LinkedIn ($69/mo extra) and calling ($29/mo extra) as add-ons. The Jason AI SDR ($800/month) can run autonomous outreach.

Reply.io's strength is its breadth: email, LinkedIn, phone, SMS, and WhatsApp, plus agency features and a solid API. The weakness is that full multichannel gets expensive fast ($187/user/month for everything). Still significantly less than Salesloft, and with transparent pricing.

5. Lemlist

Lemlist's Email Pro plan starts at $55/user/month (annual), or $69 monthly. The Multichannel Expert plan at $79/user/month (annual) adds LinkedIn automation, calling, and access to the 450M+ lead database. Both tiers went up $10/user in February 2026. The standout feature is image and video personalization in emails, which consistently drives above-average reply rates. G2 rating: 4.6/5 from 1,270+ reviews.

Compared to Salesloft, Lemlist is dramatically cheaper and faster to set up. No minimum seats, no custom quotes, no multi-month onboarding. The trade-off: no deal management, no conversation intelligence, and limited reporting. For teams under 20 reps focused on creative outbound, Lemlist delivers strong value per dollar.

Read more: Lemlist Alternatives

6. Instantly

Instantly is the budget end of this list. The Growth plan at $37.60/month (annual) gives you unlimited email accounts, unlimited warm-up, and 5,000 emails/month. No per-seat pricing. If your team does email-only outreach and Salesloft's $125+/user/month feels like overkill, Instantly cuts costs by 70-80%.

The obvious limitation: email only. No LinkedIn, no calling, no deal management. If you need multichannel sequences or enterprise features, look at Overloop, Reply.io, or Outreach instead. But for teams where cold email is the primary motion and budget matters, Instantly is a legitimate option.

Read more: Instantly Alternatives

7. HubSpot Sales Hub

HubSpot Sales Hub Starter costs $20/seat/month. Professional costs $90/seat/month (annual). If your team already runs HubSpot CRM, adding Sales Hub is the simplest migration path from Salesloft.

Sequences, templates, calling, and basic automation are all built into the CRM. No data sync issues, no separate platform to maintain. The downside is that HubSpot's outbound capabilities are less sophisticated than Salesloft's. No LinkedIn automation. Simpler sequence logic. But for teams where outbound is one part of a broader sales motion (not the entire motion), HubSpot covers the bases.

8. Groove (by Clari)

This one is ironic: Groove was already part of Clari before the Salesloft merger. It's the Salesforce-native sales engagement option within the Clari ecosystem. Pricing ranges from $50-150/user/month.

If you're on Salesforce and you want activity data to live entirely within your CRM, Groove does that better than anything else. The catch: it's now part of the same company as Salesloft, and how Clari positions these two products against each other long-term is still unclear. You could end up facing the same consolidation concerns that drove you to look at alternatives in the first place.

Making the Decision

  • Downsizing from enterprise? Overloop. Get multichannel at a fraction of the cost, set up in a day.
  • Switching at the same tier? Outreach. Direct competitor, similar capabilities.
  • Consolidating your stack? Apollo (data + outreach) or HubSpot (CRM + outreach).
  • Want creative outbound? Lemlist. Image personalization and a huge lead database.
  • Just need cheap email volume? Instantly. 70-80% cost reduction if email is enough.
  • Want AI to do the work? Overloop for sequence generation, Reply.io for full AI SDR.
  • Salesforce-first? Groove, despite the Clari overlap.

The Clari-Salesloft merger created a window where many teams are re-evaluating their stack. Use it. Most of these tools offer free trials, and testing a focused tool against an enterprise platform often reveals how much complexity you were paying for but not using.
