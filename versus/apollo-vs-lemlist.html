Key differences between Apollo and Lemlist

Data-Driven vs Creative Outreach

Apollo's 275M+ contact database and advanced filters power data-driven prospecting. Lemlist's custom images, video inserts, and visual personalization drive creative, human-centric outreach.

All-in-One vs Specialized

Apollo covers sourcing, engagement, and tracking from one dashboard. Lemlist focuses on making outreach creative and engaging but requires imported lists or external data sources.

Different Pricing Models

Apollo offers a free tier that scales with paid plans. Lemlist uses flat-rate per-seat pricing after a trial. Your volume and budget determine which is more cost-effective.

Apollo: What It Does Best

Apollo provides a powerful prospecting engine with a 275M+ contact database, robust search filters (firmographics, technographics, intent signals, job changes), and multistep automation across email, phone, and LinkedIn. It's built for data-driven sales teams that want end-to-end prospecting and engagement from one dashboard.

Limitations include basic personalization options, a steeper learning curve for small teams, and confusing pricing tiers with separate limits on leads and email sends.

Lemlist: What It Does Best

Lemlist focuses on boosting reply rates with creative personalization. Custom images, embedded videos, LinkedIn voice notes, and dynamic variables make outreach feel personal and human. The platform appeals to agencies, startups, and teams prioritizing engagement quality over volume.

Limitations include no built-in lead database (you import your own), Lemwarm only on higher tiers, and DIY technical setup that can go wrong for non-technical users.

Deliverability & Performance

Apollo focuses on verified data, sending cadences, and sender reputation tools. Lemlist takes a more active approach with automated warm-up, inbox rotation, and human-like delivery patterns. Lemlist's personalization features (images, video) also reduce template fatigue, supporting better engagement.

Pricing Comparison

Apollo offers a free tier with limitations, then paid plans that scale with database access and features. Lemlist uses flat-rate per-seat pricing after a trial period. Apollo is more cost-effective for high-volume, data-driven teams. Lemlist is more predictable for smaller teams focused on creative outreach.
