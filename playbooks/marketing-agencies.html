Messaging angles that work

These are the 3 approaches that consistently get replies in this vertical. Pick the one that fits your prospect best.

1. White-label pitch
Offer outbound as a new service line. You handle the ops, they get the meetings.
Why it works: Agencies always want new revenue streams. Outbound has 70%+ margins when white-labeled.
2. Competitor comparison
"3 of your competitors already offer outbound. Here's the playbook they use."
Why it works: FOMO + concrete proof. Nobody wants to be the agency that doesn't offer what competitors do.
3. Client results angle
Lead with a specific result: "We helped [agency] book 18 meetings/mo for their SaaS clients."
Why it works: Agencies sell results. Show them the result, they'll figure out how to sell it.

When they push back

The most common objections in this vertical and exactly what to say.

"We already do outbound internally"
Ask: how many meetings per client per month? Most agencies doing it internally manage 3-5. With a dedicated platform, their clients get 15-20. It's not about doing outbound -- it's about doing it at scale.
"Our clients wouldn't pay for outbound"
Reframe: your clients are already paying for leads through ads, SEO, events. Outbound is just another channel -- and it's the only one with predictable unit economics. Start with one client as a pilot.
"We don't have the expertise"
That's the point of white-labeling. You don't need to be an outbound expert. We handle the infrastructure, deliverability, and optimization. Your team focuses on the client relationship and messaging.
